Ownership and Outcome

Civatlas is a historical city-builder with a special twist developed for Early Access. Using historical zoning tools, the player is invested with the power to plan a renaissance metropolis. I worked as the general design lead from concept through Early Access release. My work touched systems design, production planning, progression wireframes, UI and audio prototyping,implementation support and artist reference. I was also heavily involved in day-to-day design leadership and project planning. In practice, that meant turning broad intent into usable feature direction: defining unit behavior, improving the readability of the games unique mechanics, clarifying the rules of the economy and facilitating production by reviewing execution across the team.

Responsibilities

  • Led design work from concept to Early Access release. Performed documentation tasks designing systems, production materials, technology trees and production wireframes, UI and audio prototyping, artist reference, and day-to-day feature direction across active development.
  • Advanced the project through more than 40+ UX and interface designs spanning resource display, tooltips, menus, game mechanics and task clarity, materially improving readability and player guidance.
  • Helped define and refine 12+ behaviors of the core NPC unit (civvie) and various settlement behaviors, including housing, labor assignment, job qualification logic, resource stockpiling behavior, and other automation rules that shaped how the simulation presented and played.
  • Worked closely with programmers to implement and iterate on features in-engine and moved implementation forward through economy design handling recipes, stockpiles, workshop inputs/outputs, and clarified player-facing UI.
  • Captured playtest findings, turned them into actionable design specs, and established the core prototype brief, including UX wireframes, asset lists, animation needs, and NPC-management UI.
