Position Summary

WWII bomber simulation built around historical research, mission framing, aircraft operations, and crew-space readability. My role bridged design, historical reference, narrative support, visual guidance, and cross-discipline coordination, with a strong emphasis on turning museum and archival material into implementation-ready game content for both gameplay and art production.

Selected Responsibilities

  • Collaborated with the Warplane Heritage Museum and primary-source material to ground design in historical reference.
  • Designed and documented mechanics, mission structures, and level concepts based on bomber operations, crew procedures, and period context.
  • Built reference mockups, including a full airbase layout, to help art teams work to correct scale, placement, and period detail.
  • Compiled obscure manuals, diagrams, photographs, and layout references to support artists, modellers, and environment work with period-accurate material.

Design Work Samples

This research pack reflects hours of searching for obscure reference material and compiling it into something useful for production. The goal was to give artists and modellers period-accurate references, support aircraft and crew-space detail, and provide layout material for airbase spaces and structures.

Clothing and Material Culture on Base

A short reference brief focused on clothing issue, kitchen-duty footwear, and everyday support-space material culture on base. It was assembled to give artists and modellers grounded reference for personnel presentation, support interiors, and the practical texture of airbase life beyond aircraft-only material.
