Ownership and Outcome

Historical city-builder developed toward Early Access. I worked as the general design lead from concept through Early Access preparation across systems design, production planning, wireframes, implementation support, UI and audio prototyping, artist reference, and day-to-day design leadership. In practice, that meant turning broad intent into usable feature direction: defining worker behavior, improving settlement readability, clarifying economy and production flow, reviewing execution across the team, and converting playtest or implementation friction into concrete design changes the project could act on.

Responsibilities

  • Led design work from concept to Early Access across systems, documentation, production materials, wireframes, UI and audio prototyping, artist reference, task delegation, and day-to-day feature direction across active development.
  • Advanced the project through more than 40 UX and interface improvements spanning resource display, tooltips, menus, plot actions, building readouts, and task clarity, materially improving readability and player guidance.
  • Helped define and refine 12 core civvie and settlement behaviors, including housing, labor assignment, qualification logic, stockpile behavior, and automation rules that shaped how the simulation read and played.
  • Partnered with programmers to implement and iterate features in-engine, then moved implementation forward through reproducible testing, bug follow-through, economy-flow guidance across recipes, stockpiles, workshop inputs and outputs, and clearer player-facing production visibility.
  • Captured playtest findings, turned them into actionable design changes, and established the core Civvie prototype brief, including player-facing purpose, required assets, animation needs, and labor-management UI.
