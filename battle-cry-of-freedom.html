Ownership and Outcome

Battle Cry of Freedom is a large-scale historical multiplayer shooter set during the American Civil War that supports around 200+ bots and players in combined arms combat taking place in the 19th century. My work was focused on research-driven level design. I took levels through pen and paper designs to terrain iteration, graybox, primary asset placement followed by player testing to help iterate on sightline and point control, route readability, and multiplayer flow until each level played as intended. The levels were designed for small (20) and large (sometimes 200!) population battle spaces that had to support authenticity and playability under heavy player counts.

Responsibilities

  • Researched historical battles and real-world locations to support map planning and environmental authenticity.
  • Produced paper designs, terrain roughouts, grayboxes, terrain passes, asset blockouts and final polish for multiplayer spaces and mode-specific flow.
  • Learned a proprietary editor and terrain design to create and manipulate GAEA splatmaps as the basis for map layouts.
  • Designed paths from spawn to capture points around bottlenecks, gates, through potentially destructible spaces, designed dynamic spawn zones, and accounted for sightlines.
  • Used cover, elevation, structures, ridgelines, windage and visual anchors to shape movement, combat readability, and battle rhythm under pressure.
  • Built levels around period ballistics, melee combat, vehicles, and siege-minded fortifications grounded in historical references.
  • Supported playtesting, set dressing, and QA so final maps remained both believable and playable for the time period in which the game is set.
